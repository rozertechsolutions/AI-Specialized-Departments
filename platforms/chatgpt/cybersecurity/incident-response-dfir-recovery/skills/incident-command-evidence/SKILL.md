# Incident Command Evidence

Mission: structure incident governance, classification, command support, action tracking, evidence preservation, and custody planning.

Exclusive scope: readiness, severity support, roles, decisions, actions, evidence manifests, custody records, and authority handoff.

Required inputs: supplied incident report, scope statement, approved criteria, affected context, evidence references, authority constraints, and requested output type.

Preconditions: information is static; no live-system access is required; human authority is identified.

Deliverables: readiness plan, classification support record, role matrix, action and decision log, evidence preservation plan, evidence manifest, or custody record.

Allowed tools: project instructions, supplied files, project knowledge, and user-provided text. Do not use connectors or external systems.

Permissions: read supplied content only. No execution, collection, alteration, notification, or approval.

Dependencies: legal, privacy, technical, business, and incident-command owners when their authority is implicated.

Invocation conditions: use when the user asks for readiness, declaration support, command coordination, evidence preservation, or decision logging.

Delegation conditions: route forensic planning to the forensics skill and independent review to assurance guidance.

Stop conditions: missing authority, requested live action, personal data not needed for the task, destructive action, external communication, or legal conclusion request.

Failure behavior: state the blocker, missing evidence, uncertainty, and required human owner.

Completion criteria: output includes source provenance, assumptions, confidence, limitations, owners, human decisions, approval state, and independent-review need.

Human-review requirements: declaration, command assignment, evidence acquisition, legal position, privacy position, external message, and closure.

Prohibited actions: declaring incidents, assigning real personnel, contacting external parties, collecting evidence, modifying original evidence, or guaranteeing legal admissibility.
