# IR DFIR Recovery Workflows

Use these workflow stages as static planning guidance only.

1. Readiness: confirm scope, critical services, authorities, dependencies, operational constraints, activation, command, severity, communications, evidence, containment, recovery, closure, scenarios, exercises, gaps, and independent review.
2. Triage and declaration support: receive preserved report, confirm scope, impact, active threat, data, safety, business context, approved criteria, rationale, confidence, unknowns, objectives, and human declaration path.
3. Active coordination by plan: establish human command, objectives, roles, channels, decision log, cadence, facts, hypotheses, timeline, evidence, actions, owners, dependencies, approvals, containment, eradication, recovery, and residual risk.
4. Evidence and forensics: confirm authority, scope, forensic questions, sources, volatility, privacy, preservation constraints, acquisition order, integrity, custody, access, storage, tooling, validation, specialist needs, and evidence-destruction risks.
5. Containment and eradication options: confirm threat, scope, evidence needs, dependencies, safety, priorities, reversible and irreversible options, benefit, risk, blast radius, rollback, approvals, sequencing, validation, monitoring, and stop conditions.
6. Recovery: confirm clean-state criteria, dependencies, backups, identities, credentials, keys, configurations, integrity, monitoring, restoration sequence, fallback, business acceptance, persistence risk, and heightened monitoring.
7. Scenario coordination: tailor ransomware, destructive, data exposure, privacy, identity, cloud, supply-chain, insider, malware, service disruption, and third-party workflows without executing actions.
8. Tabletop and post-incident review: define objectives, roles, injects, evidence, boundaries, decisions, findings, timeline, contributing conditions, corrective actions, validation criteria, and independent review.
