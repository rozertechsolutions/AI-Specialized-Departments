# Responsibility Model

Area 06 owns static planning and coordination artifacts for the declared-incident lifecycle. It does not own routine SOC monitoring, enterprise legal authority, public communications, regulator notification, business-continuity ownership, production administration, or offensive testing.

Domains:

1. Incident response preparedness and governance: policy support, severity model, roles, command structure, activation, communications, evidence, decision logs, handoffs, exercises, and review cadence.
2. Incident classification, scoping, and command support: facts, affected scope, identities, data, timeline, impact, safety, confidence, objectives, actions, owners, decisions, and escalation.
3. Evidence preservation and chain of custody: source list, preservation priority, legal-hold dependency, provenance, integrity, access, transfer, storage, and review requirements.
4. Digital forensics planning and analysis coordination: forensic questions, acquisition plan, volatility order, validation, contamination controls, report interpretation, and specialist handoff.
5. Containment and eradication planning: options, benefit, impact, evidence risk, safety, dependencies, rollback, approvals, sequencing, validation, and stop conditions.
6. Recovery and restoration assurance: clean-state criteria, dependency order, identity reset, credential and key rotation planning, monitoring, validation, fallback, business acceptance, residual risk, and heightened monitoring.
7. Specialized scenario coordination: ransomware, data exposure, identity, cloud, supply-chain, insider, malware, destructive, service disruption, and third-party incidents.
8. Crisis, legal, privacy, regulatory, and communications handoff: decision points, deadlines, approvals, message consistency, and separation of technical facts from legal conclusions.
9. Post-incident review and corrective action: evidence-based timeline, contributing conditions, control failures, decisions, impacts, recovery, lessons, and measurable actions.
10. Incident assurance and independent review: classification, evidence, decisions, containment plans, recovery claims, communications inputs, closure, gaps, inconsistencies, hidden residual risk, and premature closure.
