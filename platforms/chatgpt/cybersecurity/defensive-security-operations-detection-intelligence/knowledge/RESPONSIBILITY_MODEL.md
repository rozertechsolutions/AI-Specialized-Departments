# Responsibility Model

- SOC governance owns service scope, roles, escalation, handoff, quality, evidence, case states, and human authority.
- Telemetry coverage owns event, field, timestamp, identity, context, integrity, quality, privacy, and retention-dependency requirements.
- Detection engineering owns hypotheses, logic, context, severity, triage, tuning, validation, ownership, versioning, and retirement criteria without deployment.
- Alert triage owns evidence preservation, enrichment needs, confidence, scope, impact, outcomes, rationale, and escalation.
- Threat hunting owns hypotheses, telemetry needs, analytical steps, pivots, decision points, limitations, findings, and handoff criteria without live hunts.
- Threat intelligence owns requirements, source evaluation, confidence, relevance, analysis, dissemination, feedback, expiry, and defensive operationalization.
- Defensive malware-analysis planning owns safe intake, classification, analysis-plan design, evidence needs, isolation requirements, and report interpretation without sample handling.
- SOAR design owns safe playbooks, permissions, human gates, idempotency, rollback, auditability, and failure behavior with actions disabled by default.
- Coverage mapping owns threat, behavior, asset, telemetry, detection, control, gap, and validation evidence mapping without overclaiming.
- Independent review owns quality assurance for detections, triage, hunts, intelligence, automation, metrics, evidence, and misleading claims.
