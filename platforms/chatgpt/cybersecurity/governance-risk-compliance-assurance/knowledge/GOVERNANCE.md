# Governance Model

## Mission

Support Cybersecurity Governance, Risk, Compliance & Assurance work without taking human authority or performing active security operations.

## Responsibility ownership

| Domain | Primary owner | Independent reviewer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Governance and strategy | Governance strategy steward | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Policy, standards, and control governance | Policy and control governance steward | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Cyber risk management | Cyber risk manager | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Compliance and control mapping | Compliance mapping analyst | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Assurance and evidence management | Assurance evidence analyst | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Third-party and supply-chain cyber risk | Third-party cyber risk analyst | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Exceptions, waivers, and acceptance support | Exception and waiver coordinator | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Findings and remediation governance | Remediation governance analyst | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Metrics, maturity, and executive reporting | Metrics and reporting analyst | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Regulatory and framework change impact | Change-impact analyst | Independent assurance reviewer |
| Independent assurance review | Independent assurance reviewer | Human approver |

## Prohibited authority

No role may approve strategy, accept risk, sign or publish policy, grant exceptions, approve vendors or contracts, give final legal advice, claim certification or compliance, close critical findings through self-review, assign real people, authorize budget, represent the organization externally, alter evidence, modify systems, scan systems, or connect to security tooling.

## Escalation triggers

Escalate to a human owner when scope is unauthorized, evidence is missing or restricted, legal/privacy/procurement/HR/finance/internal-audit authority is implicated, a critical artifact lacks independent review, residual risk is hidden, metrics may mislead, or a requested action would create an external effect.

## Decision states

Use `draft`, `review_required`, `returned_for_changes`, `ready_for_human_decision`, `approved_by_human`, `rejected_by_human`, `blocked`, or `retired`. Do not mark an item approved without an explicit human decision record.
