# IR DFIR Recovery Workflows

All workflows are planning and review guidance only.

- Readiness review: scope, threat profile, critical services, roles, authorities, legal/privacy dependencies, constraints, activation, command, severity, communications, evidence, containment, recovery, closure, scenario coverage, exercise needs, missing authorities, and independent review.
- Incident triage/declaration support: preserved report, affected scope, impact, active threat, data, safety, business context, approved criteria, rationale, confidence, unknowns, objectives, and human declaration.
- Active coordination plan: human command, objectives, roles, channels, decision log, cadence, facts, hypotheses, timeline, evidence, actions, owners, dependencies, approvals, cross-functional coordination, residual risk.
- Evidence/forensics plan: authority, scope, questions, sources, volatility, privacy, preservation, acquisition order, integrity, custody, access, storage, tooling requirements, validation, specialists, evidence-destruction risks, and human authorization.
- Containment/eradication options: threat, scope, evidence needs, dependencies, safety, priorities, reversible and irreversible options, benefits, risks, blast radius, rollback, approval, sequencing, validation, monitoring, and stop conditions.
- Recovery plan: clean-state criteria, dependencies, backups, identities, credentials, keys, configurations, data integrity, monitoring, sequence, fallback, business acceptance, persistence risk, reinfection risk, compromised-trust risk, and residual risk.
- Scenario coordination: ransomware, destructive, data exposure, privacy, identity, cloud, supply-chain, insider, malware, third-party, legal, safety, and communications dependencies.
- Tabletop and post-incident: objectives, roles, assumptions, injects, decisions, evidence, boundaries, gaps, strengths, corrective actions, timeline, contributing conditions, control failures, root-cause limits, and independent review.
